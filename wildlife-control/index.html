Signs You Need Wildlife Control in Stonecrest, GA

Being aware of the signs of a potential wildlife issue can help you address the problem quickly before it escalates. Early detection and intervention can save you significant time, money, and stress. Ignoring these signs can lead to more extensive damage and greater risks to your health and safety.

Strange Noises in Attics, Walls, or Crawl Spaces

Scratching, rustling, thumping, or chirping sounds coming from within your home's structure are common indicators of wildlife activity. These noises are often most prominent during nesting periods or when animals are active, such as during the night. Different animals make different sounds, and our experts can often identify the culprit based on the type and location of the noise. For instance, squirrels may make scratching sounds, while raccoons might make thumping or growling noises. Squirrel removal can be complex, but we have the expertise.

Visible Damage to Your Property's Exterior

Wildlife can cause significant damage to your home's exterior as they attempt to gain entry or build nests. Look for chewed fascia boards, damaged soffits, torn screens, bent vents, or signs of digging around the foundation. Raccoons are notorious for damaging property to access attics and chimneys. These entry points not only allow wildlife in but also compromise the structural integrity and energy efficiency of your home. Prompt repair is essential after removal.

Unpleasant Odors

Animal urine, feces, and nesting materials can create persistent and strong odors that permeate your home. These odors are not only unpleasant but can also indicate significant contamination that requires professional cleanup. Deceased animals within walls or attics can also produce very strong and distinct odors that require immediate attention for removal and sanitation. Don't simply mask the smell; address the source.

Droppings or Tracks

Finding animal droppings or tracks in or around your home is a clear sign of wildlife presence. The size, shape, and location of the droppings can help identify the type of animal. Finding them in your attic, crawl space, or garage is a strong indicator of an internal infestation. Proper identification is crucial for effective removal and cleanup due to potential health risks from contaminants.

Disturbed Insulation or Wiring

Wildlife, particularly rodents and squirrels, often build nests in attics and walls, disturbing insulation and potentially chewing on electrical wiring. Damaged wiring poses a serious fire hazard. If you notice areas of compressed or torn insulation, or have experienced unexplained electrical issues, wildlife could be the cause. Addressing this quickly is vital for your safety.

Sightings of Wildlife Where They Shouldn't Be

Seeing squirrels in your attic, raccoons in your chimney, or snakes in your basement are obvious signs you need wildlife control. Even seeing animals frequently in your yard or near your home can indicate they are exploring potential entry points. Be particularly vigilant if you see animals during the day that are typically nocturnal, as this can sometimes indicate illness or a den nearby. Our services cover a wide range of wildlife, including squirrels and raccoons.

Damage to Gardens or Landscaping

If your garden is being raided, or your landscaping is being dug up, local wildlife is likely the culprit. Deer, rabbits, groundhogs, and even raccoons can cause significant damage to plants and lawns in their search for food or shelter. Identifying the type of damage can help pinpoint the animal responsible and determine appropriate deterrents or removal methods.

Pets Acting Strangely

If your pets are exhibiting unusual behavior, such as barking excessively at walls or ceilings, scratching at doors, or showing signs of agitation in specific areas of your home, it could be a reaction to the presence of wildlife. Animals have a keen sense of smell and hearing and can often detect wildlife before humans do. Pay attention to your pets' cues.

Increased Insect Activity

Sometimes, wildlife can introduce other pests into your home. Animals like raccoons and squirrels can carry fleas, ticks, and mites, which can then infest your property. If you've seen an unexplained increase in insect activity, it might be linked to a wildlife infestation. (Flea treatment and tick control are also services we offer.</p

Presence of Nests

Finding nests made of twigs, leaves, insulation, or other materials in your attic, chimney, or elsewhere on your property is a clear indication of wildlife habitation. These nests often contain young, requiring careful and humane removal to ensure the well-being of the animals and prevent them from returning. Nests can also be a source of parasites and odors.

Understanding the Importance of Professional Wildlife Control

While it might be tempting to try and handle a wildlife issue yourself, there are numerous reasons why engaging professional wildlife control is the safest and most effective approach. DIY methods can be dangerous, inhumane, and often ineffective, leading to recurring problems and potential harm to both you and the animals. Professional services offer expertise, specialized equipment, and knowledge of local regulations.

Safety Risks

Attempting to remove wildlife without proper training and equipment can expose you to significant risks. Animals may bite, scratch, or transmit diseases like rabies, hantavirus, or leptospirosis. Additionally, accessing areas like attics and crawl spaces can be hazardous due to potential structural issues, electrical hazards, or confined spaces. Our trained technicians are equipped with the necessary safety gear and protocols to minimize these risks.

Humane Handling

Ethical wildlife control prioritizes the welfare of the animals while ensuring their removal. Professionals use humane trapping methods and follow guidelines for relocation that reduce stress and increase the chances of survival for the relocated animal. Incorrect trapping or handling methods can cause unnecessary suffering to the animal and may even be illegal in some areas. We are committed to humane practices in Stonecrest, GA.

Effective and Long-Lasting Solutions

Professional wildlife control goes beyond simply removing the animal. We identify and seal entry points, address damage, and implement preventative measures to deter future infestations. This comprehensive approach ensures a more effective and long-lasting solution compared to simply removing an individual animal. We aim to solve the problem permanently.

Knowledge of Local Regulations

Wildlife management is often subject to local and state regulations regarding trapping, relocation, and specific species. Professional services are knowledgeable about these regulations, ensuring that all activities are conducted legally and responsibly. Attempting DIY methods without knowledge of these laws could result in fines or legal issues.

Preventing Property Damage

Wildlife can cause extensive and costly damage to your property, from chewing through electrical wires to contaminating insulation and damaging structural components. Professional removal and exclusion can prevent further damage, saving you money on expensive repairs down the line. Early intervention is key to minimizing damage.

Health Risks Associated with Wildlife Presence

Wildlife can carry a variety of parasites and diseases that can be transmitted to humans and pets. Their droppings and urine can contain harmful bacteria and viruses. Professional cleaning and sanitation are essential to mitigate these health risks after wildlife removal. We use specialized products to ensure your home is safe and healthy again.
