Addressing Common Signs You Need Squirrel Removal

Recognizing the early signs of a squirrel problem can prevent more extensive damage and costly repairs down the line. Being aware of these indicators is the first step towards a squirrel-free property. Many people initially dismiss these signs, but taking prompt action is crucial.

Noises in the Attic or Walls

Hearing scratching, scurrying, chattering, or gnawing sounds, especially in the early morning or late afternoon, is one of the most common indicators of squirrel activity. These noises originate from squirrels nesting, moving around, or chewing on materials within your home's structure.

These sounds can be incredibly unsettling and disruptive to your peace and quiet. Squirrels in these spaces often build nests using insulation and other readily available materials, which can lead to significant damage over time. The constant movement and vocalizations are a clear signal that unwelcome guests have taken up residence. Ignoring these noises will only allow the problem to escalate, leading to more extensive damage and a larger, more complex removal process. Our experts can pinpoint the source of these sounds during an inspection.

Chewed Wires or Damaged Insulation

Squirrels have a natural need to chew to keep their teeth from growing too long. Unfortunately, electrical wires and insulation are often appealing targets. Chewed wires can create serious fire hazards, while damaged insulation reduces your home's energy efficiency.

The consequences of squirrels chewing on wires are particularly alarming. Damaged wiring can lead to power outages, short circuits, and even house fires. The risk to your property and the safety of your family is substantial. Beyond the fire risk, damaged insulation compromises your home's ability to maintain comfortable temperatures, leading to higher energy bills. This type of damage is often hidden from view, making professional inspection essential. Our team can assess the extent of the damage and recommend necessary repairs.

Visible Entry and Exit Points

Look for openings around your roofline, eaves, vents, chimneys, and siding where squirrels might be entering or exiting your home. These openings may show signs of gnawing or damage around the edges.

Squirrels are agile climbers and can exploit surprisingly small gaps and holes. Over time, they can enlarge these openings through persistent chewing, making it easier for more pests to enter. Visible entry and exit points confirm the presence of squirrels and provide valuable information for our removal strategy. Sealing these points effectively is a critical step in preventing future infestations. Addressing damaged vents or loose siding is often necessary. For more information on preventing pests, you can visit our home page.

Droppings or Urine Stains

Finding squirrel droppings (small, pellet-like) or urine stains in your attic, walls, or other areas of your home is a clear indication of an infestation. These can pose health risks and create unpleasant odors.

Squirrel droppings and urine can contain bacteria and pathogens that are harmful to human health. In enclosed spaces like attics, the accumulation of waste can lead to strong, unpleasant odors that permeate your home. This not only creates an unsanitary environment but also deteriorates the air quality within your living spaces. Professional cleanup and deodorization are often necessary after squirrel removal to ensure a healthy environment. Effective pest management includes addressing the aftermath of the infestation.

Nesting Materials

Discovering nesting materials like leaves, twigs, insulation, or shredded paper in your attic or other enclosed spaces confirms that squirrels have established a home within your Walls.

Squirrels build nests to protect themselves and their young, often in quiet, secluded areas of your home like the attic or wall voids. The presence of nesting materials indicates a more established infestation, possibly including a litter of young squirrels. Removing nesting materials and thoroughly cleaning the affected area is an important part of the removal and prevention process. This also helps identify the extent of the infestation and the amount of cleanup required. We also offer services for rodent control which can share similar signs.

Seeing Squirrels Entering or Exiting

The most obvious sign is witnessing squirrels repeatedly entering or exiting your property through specific openings. This observation directly confirms their presence and activity.

Seeing squirrels come and go from your home, especially at consistent times of the day, removes all doubt about an infestation. Noting the location of these entry and exit points is incredibly helpful for our technicians during the inspection phase. This visual confirmation allows us to quickly focus on the primary areas of concern and implement targeted removal strategies. It also highlights the frequency of their access to your property.

While not directly inside your home, damage to bird feeders, gardens, or outdoor structures can be a sign of active squirrel presence on your property, indicating a higher likelihood of indoor intrusion.

If squirrels are already active and comfortable on your property, foraging for food and causing damage outdoors, the transition to seeking shelter inside your home becomes much more probable. Addressing outdoor attractants can be an important part of a comprehensive squirrel management plan. This includes securing trash cans and removing fallen fruit from trees. Presence of Other Pests

Areas frequented by squirrels can sometimes attract other pests like fleas, ticks, or mites that may drop off the squirrels and infest your home.

Squirrels can carry unwanted hitchhikers. If squirrels have been nesting in your attic, you may find yourself also dealing with a secondary infestation of fleas or ticks that were living on the squirrels. This adds another layer of a pest problem that needs to be addressed after the squirrels are removed. Our services can help with flea treatment and tick control as well.

Unpleasant Odors

A persistent, foul odor coming from walls, attics, or chimneys could be the result of squirrel urine, droppings, or even a deceased animal.

As mentioned before, the accumulation of squirrel waste creates strong, unpleasant odors. In addition, if a squirrel becomes trapped and dies within a wall void or other hidden area, the decomposition process will produce a very distinct and offensive smell. These odors are not only unpleasant but can also attract other pests. Professional removal and cleaning are necessary to eliminate these odors effectively. Addressing the source of the odor is key to resolving the issue entirely.

Addressing Specific Types of Squirrels

While the core principles of squirrel removal are similar, understanding the behaviors of common squirrel species in our area can help in identifying and addressing the problem effectively. Different species may have slightly different nesting preferences or activity patterns.

Eastern Gray Squirrels

These are the most common squirrels you'll encounter in Stonecrest, GA. They are highly adaptable and frequently seek shelter in attics and wall voids. Their tendency to bury nuts can sometimes lead to them attempting to enter homes through loose soil around foundations. We have extensive experience dealing with Eastern Gray Squirrels.

Flying Squirrels

Less common than gray squirrels, flying squirrels are nocturnal and often enter attics through smaller gaps than their larger counterparts. Their presence is typically indicated by sounds at night and smaller droppings. Identifying flying squirrels requires a different approach due to their nocturnal nature and smaller size.

Understanding which type of squirrel you are dealing with can help tailor the removal approach for maximum effectiveness and minimal impact. Our experts are trained to identify the specific species infesting your property. Similarly, identifying the type of insect is important for treatments like ant control or cockroach control.

Preventing Future Squirrel Infestations

Removing the current squirrel problem is only part of the solution. Implementing preventative measures is crucial to avoid future issues. Our team provides recommendations and services to help squirrel-proof your home effectively.

Sealing Entry Points

This is the most important step in prevention. We identify and seal all potential entry points, no matter how small, using durable materials that squirrels cannot easily chew through. This includes repairing damaged vents, installing chimney caps, and sealing gaps around fascia boards and eaves.

Trimming Tree Branches

Squirrels use overhanging tree branches to gain access to roofs and upper levels of your home. Trimming branches back away from your house makes it more difficult for squirrels to reach potential entry points. This simple step can significantly reduce the risk of intrusion.

Securing Food Sources

Removing easily accessible food sources like bird feeders and unsecured trash cans can make your property less attractive to squirrels. Storing pet food in airtight containers also helps. Reducing attractive food sources minimizes the incentive for squirrels to frequent your property. For other wildlife concerns visit our wildlife control page.
